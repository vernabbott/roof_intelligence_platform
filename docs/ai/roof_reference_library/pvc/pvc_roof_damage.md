# PVC Roof Damage Identification

## Description

This file provides a starting reference for identifying and documenting visible damage on polyvinyl chloride (PVC) roof surfaces. PVC is a reinforced thermoplastic single-ply membrane with heat-welded seams and is commonly white or light colored. Damage observations may include punctures, tears, open welds, plasticizer loss, surface cracking, exposed reinforcement, shrinkage, deformation, deteriorated flashing, and failed patches.

## Why It's Important

PVC roofing depends on a continuous membrane, sound heat-welded seams, and secure flashing and termination details. Aging, chemical exposure, foot traffic, impact, thermal movement, and incompatible repair materials can weaken the membrane or its seams. Early identification can reduce moisture intrusion, protect insulation and decking, support targeted repairs, and improve condition and remaining-service-life estimates.

## Visual Characteristics

### Aging

- Fading, yellowing, chalking, surface erosion, or loss of the original finish
- Fine cracking, crazing, brittleness, or exposed reinforcement caused by plasticizer loss
- Deteriorated welds, sealants, patches, flashings, and termination details
- Concentrated abrasion and wear along walk paths and service areas

### Ponding

- Standing water around drains, equipment, curbs, seams, and depressed areas
- Dark staining, sediment rings, algae, biological growth, and accumulated dirt
- Blocked drains or scuppers and water retained after adjacent areas have dried
- Membrane discoloration, wrinkling, or accelerated deterioration within ponding zones

### Leaking

- Punctures, holes, open seams, failed welds, or lifted patch edges
- Loose or separated flashing around penetrations, walls, curbs, and roof edges
- Staining, repeated repairs, or deterioration concentrated around a suspected entry point
- Soft, sunken, swollen, or irregular areas that may indicate wet insulation

### Hail Damage

- Circular bruises, dimples, fractures, cuts, or punctures in the membrane
- Concentrated impact patterns visible under low-angle lighting
- Cracked membrane where hail struck rigid substrate, fasteners, or insulation joints
- Dents and impact marks on adjacent flashings, vents, drains, and equipment

### Deformation

- Wrinkles, ridges, buckling, bubbling, blisters, or loose membrane areas
- Bridging or tenting at curbs, penetrations, perimeters, and changes in plane
- Depressions or raised areas associated with insulation movement or trapped moisture
- Membrane shrinkage or tension around seams, edges, and rooftop components

### Splitting

- Linear cracks, cuts, tears, or full-depth membrane openings
- Open, lifted, separated, or irregular heat-welded seams
- Stress splits at corners, drains, penetrations, insulation joints, and transitions
- Cracked patches or flashings with exposed reinforcement or separated edges

## AI Confidence Factors

AI confidence should reflect image quality and whether damage can be distinguished from normal welds, surface texture, dirt, glare, and temporary moisture.

### High Confidence

- A membrane opening, exposed reinforcement, failed weld, or displaced flashing is clearly visible
- The feature has distinct edges, depth, separation, or material loss
- Multiple views confirm the condition and surrounding roof context
- Image resolution distinguishes a defect from a normal seam or repair
- The condition follows a mechanically plausible damage pattern

### Medium Confidence

- A seam, patch, crack, or deformation is visible but not fully resolved
- Lighting, glare, staining, dirt, or an oblique angle affects interpretation
- The image suggests damage but does not show its complete extent
- Only one image or viewing angle is available

### Low Confidence

- The suspected damage is blurry, distant, heavily shadowed, or obstructed
- A line or color change could be a normal weld, stain, reflection, or image artifact
- Surface variation could be dirt, moisture, coating, or aging rather than damage
- The membrane type or affected component cannot be confirmed

Low- and medium-confidence findings should be verified through closer imagery and an on-site inspection.

## Severity

Severity should consider membrane continuity, seam and flashing involvement, exposed reinforcement, moisture evidence, defect extent, and location.

### Minor

Localized cosmetic fading, light abrasion, or a small stable wrinkle with no membrane opening, failed weld, exposed reinforcement, or leak indicator. Monitor during routine inspection.

### Moderate

A localized loose patch, developing weld concern, moderate wrinkle, surface cracking, or deteriorated sealant that may progress if left uncorrected. Schedule professional evaluation.

### Severe

A confirmed puncture, tear, open weld, exposed reinforcement, significant shrinkage, failed flashing, or widespread cracking with a high likelihood of moisture entry. Prioritize prompt repair.

### Critical

Extensive membrane rupture, widespread seam or flashing failure, active water intrusion, or large areas pulled away from terminations. Escalate for urgent professional assessment and temporary protection.
