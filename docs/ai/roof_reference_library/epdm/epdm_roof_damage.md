# EPDM Roof Damage Identification

## Description

This file provides a starting reference for identifying and documenting visible damage on ethylene propylene diene monomer (EPDM) roof surfaces. EPDM is a flexible synthetic-rubber membrane, commonly black but also available in reinforced and white-faced configurations. Damage observations may include punctures, tears, seam separation, shrinkage, bridging, wrinkles, surface oxidation, deteriorated flashing, and failed patches or adhesives.

## Why It's Important

EPDM relies on a continuous membrane and properly bonded seams, flashings, and accessories to keep water out of the roof assembly. Aging, shrinkage, foot traffic, sharp debris, incompatible materials, and movement at penetrations can weaken these details. Early identification can prevent moisture from reaching insulation and decking, reduce repair scope, and improve the reliability of roof condition and remaining-service-life assessments.

## Visual Characteristics

### Aging

- Surface chalking, crazing, cracking, fading, or loss of the black surface layer
- Brittle membrane, worn traffic areas, and exposed reinforcement
- Shrinkage pulling the membrane away from walls, curbs, edges, and penetrations
- Deteriorated lap adhesive, flashing, sealant, patches, and termination details

### Ponding

- Standing water in low areas, around drains, or beside rooftop equipment
- Algae, biological growth, sediment rings, and dark staining after water evaporates
- Blocked drains or visible depressions caused by deck or insulation settlement
- Wrinkles, loose membrane, or accelerated surface deterioration within ponding areas

### Leaking

- Open or fish-mouthed seams, punctures, holes, and failed patch edges
- Split or stretched membrane around drains, pipes, corners, and equipment curbs
- Soft, sunken, swollen, or discolored areas that may indicate wet insulation
- Repeated repairs, staining, or deteriorated flashing near a suspected entry point

### Hail Damage

- Circular bruises, dimples, cuts, punctures, or fractures in the rubber membrane
- Concentrated impact patterns visible under low-angle lighting
- Damage to vents, metal flashings, coatings, and other exposed rooftop components
- Cracking or membrane breaks where hail struck rigid substrate or aged, brittle EPDM

### Deformation

- Wrinkles, ridges, bubbles, blisters, tenting, or loose membrane areas
- Bridging where the membrane spans changes in plane instead of remaining bonded
- Membrane shrinkage or tension around perimeters and penetrations
- Depressions or raised areas associated with displaced or moisture-damaged insulation

### Splitting

- Linear tears, cracks, cuts, or openings through the membrane
- Seam separation with lifted, curled, or irregular lap edges
- Stress splits at corners, penetrations, drains, and changes in plane
- Cracked or separated patches and flashing membranes

## AI Confidence Factors

AI confidence should reflect image clarity and whether the feature can be distinguished from normal seams, patches, dirt, and surface texture.

### High Confidence

- A tear, opening, exposed substrate, lifted seam, or displaced flashing is clearly visible
- The defect shows distinct separation, depth, curling, or material loss
- Multiple views confirm the condition and its location
- Image resolution clearly separates a defect from a normal lap seam or repair patch
- Surrounding context supports a mechanically plausible damage pattern

### Medium Confidence

- A seam or patch appears lifted, but the edge detail is not fully resolved
- Wrinkling, shrinkage, or surface cracking is visible but its extent is uncertain
- Glare, moisture, dirt, or an oblique angle affects interpretation
- Only one image or viewing angle is available

### Low Confidence

- The suspected damage is blurry, distant, shadowed, or obstructed
- A dark line could be a normal seam, wrinkle, cable, stain, or membrane split
- Surface color variation could be oxidation, dirt, moisture, or an image artifact
- The membrane type or defect location cannot be confirmed

Low- and medium-confidence findings should be verified through closer imagery, gentle probing by a qualified roofer, or an on-site inspection.

## Severity

Severity should consider whether the membrane is open, the defect location, seam and flashing involvement, moisture evidence, and the extent of shrinkage or deterioration.

### Minor

Localized cosmetic oxidation, light abrasion, or a small stable wrinkle with no opening, exposed reinforcement, failed seam, or leak indicator. Monitor during routine inspection.

### Moderate

A localized loose patch, developing seam concern, moderate wrinkle, early shrinkage, deteriorated sealant, or abrasion that may compromise the membrane if left uncorrected. Schedule professional repair evaluation.

### Severe

A confirmed puncture, tear, open seam, exposed reinforcement, significant shrinkage, failed flashing, or widespread cracking with a high likelihood of moisture entry. Prioritize prompt repair.

### Critical

Extensive membrane rupture, widespread seam or flashing failure, active water intrusion, large areas pulled away from terminations, or damage affecting immediate weather protection. Escalate for urgent professional assessment and temporary protection.
